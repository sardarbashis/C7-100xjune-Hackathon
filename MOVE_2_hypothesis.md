# Move 2 — The Hypothesis (pre-registered, before any code)

**Project:** Concept Check — Track A · 100x Cohort 7 Code Path Hackathon

Committed before any application code is written.

---

## The Claim

Many learners can correctly define technical concepts but cannot derive them from first principles.

A learner who appears to understand a systems-thinking concept will often fail when asked deeper questions such as:

- Why does it exist?
- What problem does it solve?
- What breaks if it disappears?
- How would you rebuild the concept from scratch?

The Concept Check tool will identify the exact point where understanding becomes a memorized label and help the learner bridge that gap through guided questioning.

---

## The Three Numbers (Pre-registered)

| Outcome | The Number |
|----------|------------|
| **WIN — proves me right** | 1 or 2 out of 2 learners fail the first derivation attempt but successfully derive the concept after guided questioning |
| **NO PROBLEM EXISTS** | 2 out of 2 learners can already derive the concept correctly before intervention |
| **KILL-NUMBER — I admit I was wrong** | 0 out of 2 learners show any meaningful derivation gap and all participants reason correctly from first principles immediately |

---

## What Counts

A **Derivation Gap** exists when:

- The learner can define the concept.
- The learner can repeat a textbook explanation.
- The learner cannot explain why the concept exists.
- The learner cannot explain what breaks without it.
- The learner cannot rebuild the idea from first principles.
- The learner relies on memorized labels, analogies, or buzzwords.

Examples:

- "API is a bridge."
- "Backend stores data."
- "Database stores information."

without explaining the underlying reason these systems exist.

---

A **Gap is Closed** when:

- The learner successfully answers the follow-up question.
- The learner explains the concept using cause-and-effect reasoning.
- The learner identifies the underlying problem the concept solves.
- The learner derives the concept independently rather than recalling a definition.

## Success Criteria

The hypothesis will be considered supported if:

- At least one participant demonstrates a clear derivation gap.
- The tool successfully identifies that gap.
- Guided questioning helps the learner move from memorized explanations to causal reasoning.

The hypothesis will be considered weak if:

- Learners consistently derive concepts correctly without intervention.
- No meaningful derivation gaps are discovered.

---

## Honest Note

The biggest risk is that learners already possess deeper understanding than expected.

If all participants can consistently derive concepts from first principles without assistance, the hypothesis will be considered wrong and reported honestly.

The goal of this project is not to prove the hypothesis at any cost, but to discover whether a measurable gap exists between recognition and true understanding.
