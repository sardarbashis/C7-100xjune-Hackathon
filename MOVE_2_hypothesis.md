
# Move 2 — The Hypothesis (pre-registered, before any code)

Project: Concept Check — Track A · 100x Cohort 7 Code Path Hackathon

Committed before any application code is written.

---

## The claim

A learner who can correctly define a systems-thinking concept will still fail to derive it under at least one sharp follow-up question more often than not.

The tool will identify the exact point where understanding becomes a memorized label and help the learner close that gap.

---

## The three numbers (pre-registered, out of 2 learners tested)

| Outcome | The number |
|----------|-----------|
| WIN — proves me right | 1 or 2 of 2 learners fail the first derivation attempt but successfully derive the concept after intervention |
| NO PROBLEM EXISTS | 0 of 2 learners can already derive the concept correctly before intervention |
| KILL-NUMBER — I admit I was wrong | 0 of 2 learners show any meaningful derivation gap |

---

## What counts

A derivation gap exists when:

- The learner can define the concept.
- The learner cannot explain why it exists.
- The learner cannot explain what breaks without it.
- The learner cannot derive it from first principles.

A gap is closed when:

- The learner successfully answers the follow-up question.
- The learner can explain the concept causally rather than memorizing a definition.

## Honest note

The biggest risk is that learners already possess deeper understanding than expected.

If both participants derive concepts correctly before intervention, the hypothesis will be considered wrong and reported honestly.
